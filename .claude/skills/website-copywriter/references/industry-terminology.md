## Phrases to Avoid (Literal Translations)

### English phrases that don't work literally in German:

| English | ❌ Literal German | ✓ Better German |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Game-changer | Spielveränderer | Revolutionär / Bahnbrechend |
| Low-hanging fruit | Niedrig hängende Früchte | Schnelle Erfolge / Einfache Gewinne |
| Think outside the box | Außerhalb der Box denken | Über den Tellerrand schauen |
| Win-win situation | Gewinn-Gewinn Situation | Win-Win-Situation (used as-is) |
| No-brainer | Kein-Hirn-er | Selbstverständlich / Logische Wahl |
| Best practice | Beste Praxis | Best Practice (used as-is) |
| State-of-the-art | Stand der Kunst | Modernster Stand / Hochmodern |
| User base | Benutzerbasis | Nutzerschaft / Kundenstamm |

### German phrases that don't work literally in English:

| German | ❌ Literal English | ✓ Better English |

## Regional Variations

### German-speaking markets differ:

**Germany (Deutschland):**
- More direct in business communication
- Accepts more Anglicisms in tech
- Expects detailed product info

**Austria (Österreich):**
- Slightly more formal
- Some vocabulary differences (e.g., "Jänner" for January)
- Service-oriented language

**Switzerland (Schweiz):**
- More formal and conservative language
- Fewer Anglicisms preferred
- "Grüezi" instead of "Hallo"
- Some unique terms (e.g., "Velo" for bicycle)

## Quick Tips

1. **When in doubt, use the English term** for tech concepts (API, Cloud, etc.)
2. **Always translate** common business words (customer, price, product)
3. **Check context** for words like "performance" and "solution" 
4. **Consider your audience** - B2C can be more casual, B2B remains professional
5. **Be consistent** - pick one term and stick with it throughout the text
